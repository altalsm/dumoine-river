About the Dumoine Tote Road Trail

The Dumoine River in Western Quebec is one of the last free flowing, undammed, unsettled wild river tributaries left in the Ottawa River Watershed. This is unique, especially for a river that has had human habitation, exploration and exploitation for over a thousand years. The Quebec government protected the Dumoine River corridor as an Aquatic Reserve in 2009.

Today for the first time in sixty years you can hike the lower Dumoine as well as canoe it. A group of volunteers called Friends of Dumoine has cut open the entire tote road trail and established twenty wilderness campsites, many on the old river drive stopping places, to make the trail a wonderful hiking experience.

Partners Wolf Lake First Nation, Zec Dumoine, Canadian Parks and Wilderness Society and Friends of Dumoine are working hard to research the history of the Dumoine Band Families, the ancient portages, the stopping places, the fire towers and shanty camps and the natural highlights of the trail to bring it all to life for you when you choose to visit the trail in person or virtually on this website.

Please visit us often and support our work.

The vision

“The Dumoine Valley has been my work and playground for over fifty years. Since 1969 I have tried to visit it at least once each season.The creation of this trail is a great contribution to allow another way travellers and guides can enjoy and interpret the Dumoine River's history and natural environment... Imagine yourself in the company of 19th century travellers using this tote road, pointing out the places they stayed and the work they did with things they left behind. Discover blazes, lost relics, old campsites, squared timbers, tools and building foundations as you walk with them through history."

- Wally Schaber Friends of the Dumoine

Canadian Parks and Wilderness Society - Ottawa Valley Chapter

The Ottawa Valley Chapter of the Canadian Parks and Wilderness Society (CPAWS-OV) is proud to support the development and ongoing maintenance of the Dumoine Tote Road Trail. Starting in the early 2000's, CPAWS-OV led the campaign to have the Dumoine River protected from industrial development, culminating in the announcement by the Government of Quebec in 2008 of 1445 square kilometers being set aside a projected Aquatic Reserve which will safeguard the Dumoine watershed's rich ecological, cultural and recreational values for current and future generations.

Friends of Dumoine River

The Dumoine Tote Road Trail highlights the incredible things that can be accomplished when groups of people come together to support a cause. The Friends of the Dumoine River are a group of hardy volunteers that have worked hard to ensure a memorable and pleasant trail experience by clearing the trail, placing signs and even bringing in Thunderboxes by canoe! Since we started the reopening of the Dumoine Tote Road Trail in 2019 it has been a labour of love by many volunteers proud to call themselves Friends of Rivière du Moine.
